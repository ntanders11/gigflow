# Competitive Landscape: Musician CRM / Booking Tools

*Research Date: 2026-03-22*

---

## Executive Summary

**There is a significant gap in the market.** No single product combines all five core features: venue discovery, outbound campaigns, CRM pipeline tracking, booking calendar, and public profile with pricing. The closest competitor (Gig App) is early-stage and mobile-first.

---

## Direct Competitors (Have Some Outbound/Discovery Features)

### 1. Gig App (gig.app) - CLOSEST COMPETITOR
- **What:** AI-powered venue matching + automated outbound email campaigns
- **Pricing:** Pay-per-use (only pay for emails sent). EPK creation free. ~$6.50/campaign reported.
- **Strengths:** Algorithmic venue matching, personalized automated pitches, claims 20% response rate
- **Weaknesses:** Early-stage, mobile-first, limited reviews, unclear CRM depth
- **Threat Level:** Medium - closest to our concept but appears nascent

### 2. Booking-Agent.io
- **What:** Venue & talent buyer contact search engine with AI matching
- **Pricing:** Starting at $49.99/month
- **Strengths:** Strong venue discovery, map-based tour planning, verified contacts
- **Weaknesses:** Research tool only - no outbound campaigns, no pipeline tracking
- **Threat Level:** Low - complementary tool, not a full solution

### 3. Band Practice CRM (bandpracticecrm.com)
- **What:** CRM with 45,000+ venue database, pipeline tracking, tour logistics
- **Pricing:** 7-day free trial (likely $20-50/month)
- **Strengths:** Real CRM with venue database, lead tracking from discovery to stage
- **Weaknesses:** No automated outbound campaigns
- **Threat Level:** Medium - has CRM + venue data but missing outreach automation

### 4. Indie on the Move (indieonthemove.com)
- **What:** U.S. venue database with QuickPitch batch outreach feature
- **Pricing:** Free/Premium/Deluxe tiers (~$10-25/month)
- **Strengths:** Extensive U.S. venue database, community reviews, QuickPitch for batch emails
- **Weaknesses:** No CRM pipeline, no booking calendar, limited tracking
- **Threat Level:** Low-Medium - partial overlap

## Booking Management Tools (No Outbound Features)

### 5. Gigwell (gigwell.com)
- **What:** End-to-end booking management, Tour IQ venue database
- **Pricing:** $49/month + $99 setup (Artist), $99+/month (Agency)
- **Notes:** Enterprise-grade, targets agencies not indie artists. Tour IQ is great but no outbound.

### 6. Back On Stage (backonstageapp.com)
- **What:** Band business management - CRM, contracts, invoicing, musician scheduling
- **Pricing:** Free / $19 / $49 / $99 per month
- **Notes:** 5,000+ bands. Manages existing gigs, not prospecting. No venue database.

### 7. Giggio (getgiggio.com)
- **What:** CRM for performers - booking tracking, invoicing, contracts
- **Pricing:** ~$23/month (Pro)
- **Notes:** UK-based. Admin tool for existing bookings. No outreach.

### 8. Band Pencil (bandpencil.com)
- **What:** Gig details, contracts, invoicing, musician availability
- **Pricing:** Standard/Premium/Ultimate tiers, 14-day trial
- **Notes:** UK-based. Management tool, no prospecting features.

### 9. GigPlanner (gigplanner.com)
- **What:** Online agenda, CRM, task management
- **Pricing:** Starting at ~$5/month
- **Notes:** European-focused. Calendar/scheduling only.

## Marketplaces (Inbound Only - You List, They Find You)

### 10. GigSalad (gigsalad.com)
- 110,000+ entertainers listed. Primarily private events (weddings, parties). $10-15/month.

### 11. The Bash / GigMasters (thebash.com)
- Similar to GigSalad. Private event marketplace.

### 12. gigmit (gigmit.com)
- 15,000+ festivals/venues globally. Apply-to-play model. ~$19/month. Mixed reviews.

### 13. AmptUp (amptup.com)
- Two-sided marketplace. Venues find musicians. Growing but primarily venue-facing.

### 14. Sonicbids (sonicbids.com)
- Historically major platform. Currently relaunching ("New Platform Almost Here").

## Our Differentiators

1. **Zone-based territory management** - no one else has this. Musicians touring or playing multiple markets can organize by geography.
2. **Web scraping + enrichment** - automated venue qualification (does this place actually book live music?) goes beyond just a static database.
3. **Full pipeline in one tool** - discover > qualify > pitch > track > book > manage, all in one place.
4. **Built for the solo gigging musician** - not agencies, not private event marketplace, not enterprise.

## What Musicians Do Today

Most indie musicians cobble together:
- Google Sheets (venue contacts, outreach tracking, follow-up dates)
- Gmail (manual email outreach, one at a time)
- Bandzoogle or Linktree (public profile / EPK)
- Google Calendar (booking calendar)
- 10+ hours/week on booking admin

The spreadsheet approach is extremely common: Date / City / Venue / Contact / Status / Follow-up.
